SciCafe: Warm-Blooded Fishes — Why So Few? The Ocean's Heated Rivalry
FREE
American Museum of Natural History · 7:00 PM

Marine biologist Fernando Melendez Vazquez presents new research on why only 42 of 38,000+ fish species evolved warm-bloodedness — revealing the first evidence of an evolutionary arms race between tunas, billfishes, great white sharks and marine mammals. Built the largest evolutionary tree of fishes to date. 21+, free with RSVP.
